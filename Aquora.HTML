Smart Water Monitoring for a Sustainable Future

Aquora is an autonomous environmental robotics system designed to monitor, analyze, and report water quality conditions in real time. It focuses on critical parameters such as pH levels and turbidity to detect pollution and support ecosystem protection efforts.

The Problem

Water pollution is a growing global issue affecting rivers, lakes, and oceans. Industrial waste, chemicals, and micro-particles reduce water quality, damage marine ecosystems, and threaten biodiversity. Many polluted areas are not monitored regularly, which delays response and increases environmental damage.

Our Solution

Aquora introduces an automated robotic monitoring system that collects and analyzes water data without human intervention. It works alongside a secondary system (Aqua Robot) to enable coordinated environmental response actions.

Aquora Unit

The primary robot navigates to water sources and performs real-time analysis using pH and turbidity sensors. It continuously collects environmental data to evaluate water safety conditions.

Data Processing System

Sensor readings are processed onboard to determine water quality status. The system identifies abnormal conditions and prepares structured data for transmission.

Aqua Robot Communication

Processed data is transmitted wirelessly to the Aqua robot. This enables coordinated action such as water exchange, alerts, or environmental response decisions.
