Aquora System

We all love summer beaches and clear oceans — but beneath the surface, a silent problem is growing. Water quality is changing in ways most people never see until it’s too late.

High turbidity and unstable pH levels reduce sunlight penetration, damaging coral reefs, underwater plants, and the marine food chain that depends on them.

Who is affected

Coral reefs, underwater plants, fish species, and entire marine ecosystems.

The problem

Damage happens silently. By the time it’s visible, ecosystems are already affected.

Why it matters

Oceans regulate climate, food chains, and biodiversity. Small changes have large consequences.

Our system

Aquora uses two connected components — Scout and AquaTalkie — to monitor water conditions in real time.

Scout moves through water environments and measures pH levels and turbidity. AquaTalkie receives the data instantly and translates it into readable alerts and feedback.

Scout

Mobile sensor unit that explores water environments and collects live readings.

AquaTalkie

Receives Scout data and converts it into real-time alerts and insights.

Result

A fast monitoring loop that helps detect water issues before they become damage.
